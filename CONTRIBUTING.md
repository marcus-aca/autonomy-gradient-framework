# Contributing

Thanks for contributing to the Autonomy Gradient framework.

## What You Can Submit

1. Model language improvements
- Term clarifications
- Keyword additions
- Definition precision updates

2. Blog submissions
- Field notes
- Practical implementation patterns
- Real journey retrospectives

## How to Improve Acceptance Odds

- Keep submissions concrete and experience-based.
- Align terms to `model/terms.yml`.
- Show tradeoffs, constraints, and what changed over time.
- Prefer practical evidence over abstract claims.

## Blog Submission Workflow

1. Create a new Markdown file in `content/blogs/` using `content/blogs/_template.md`.
2. Open a pull request using the PR template.
3. Include concrete experience and avoid confidential or personal data.
4. Expect editorial review and possible revision requests.

## Content Requirements

- Must align to canonical model language in `model/terms.yml`.
- Must be original and lawful to publish.
- Must not include private, regulated, or confidential information.
- Must avoid unverifiable hard metrics unless backed by public evidence.

## Linking and Attribution Policy

External links are allowed, including links to your own resources, when they improve the reader's understanding.

Allowed:
- Relevant links to supporting documentation, repositories, case studies, or technical references.
- Links to your own related resource when it directly supports the submission.

Limits:
- Keep links focused and minimal.
- If including self-promotional links, use at most two.
- Include a short disclosure when the linked resource is your employer, product, or client work.

Not allowed:
- Affiliate, referral, or pay-per-click links.
- Tracking-heavy URLs where parameters are not required for destination routing.
- Links that are misleading, primarily promotional, or unrelated to the submission.

Maintainers may edit, relabel, or remove links during review.

## Review Expectations

- Maintainers target an initial review response within five business days.
- Review outcomes may include `accept`, `request changes`, or `decline`.
- Accepted submissions may be edited for clarity, terminology alignment, and consistency.
